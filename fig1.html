Here we can see a direct comparison between latitude and maximum temperature for 1/05/17 in each of the 546 cities. The data skews more towards the northern hemisphere, with a significant increase in data representation for cities above a latitude of 20 compared the equivalent southern distance from the equator beyond -20. Nonetheless, we can see that there is a much quicker drop in temperature as you travel north of the equator compared to south, as demonstrated by the recorded temperatures for the cities in the 20 to 40 latitude region vs cities in the -20 to -40 latitude region.
